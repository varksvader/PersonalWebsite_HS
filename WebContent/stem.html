If you didn't already know, STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math. Mass Academy is very STEM-focused. Everything comes back to STEM. Mass Academy gives us the opportunity to choose an individual project topic and work on it for the majority of A, B, and C term. We have mini-STEM fairs to show what we have done and can compete in the regional, state, and national fairs as well.

The main teacher who teaches us STEM is Ms. Curran. She teaches techniques that would help us in our STEM project, such as how to write papers and how to organize our board. STEM projects are your own individual work, which is great because you get to do something you want to do. We also get advisors who help us with our project more in depth. My advisor is Mrs. Taricco.

My project is about electricity conservation. Consumers often waste electricity because they use their appliances inefficiently, contributing to the spread of global warming. I engineered a consumer extension of smart grid, a device which accurately monitors the electricity consumption of household appliances and educates consumers on how to use electricity efficiently. Smart grid is an innovative technology which helps utility companies monitor electricity generation and consumption and promotes the use of renewable energy in households to reduce the consumption of fossil fuels.

The device, featuring a microcontroller and a current circuit, measures the consumerâ€™s electricity consumption safely and non-intrusively from the appliance wire. I also developed an app with the device to entice consumers to learn about their electricity consumption, such as a preferred amount of electricity to use and optimal times to use their appliances to minimize fossil fuel consumption. The device was tested on a variety of appliances with different energy efficiencies. I compared the measurements obtained by the device to measurements obtained from commercially available instruments. My device is extremely accurate.

When utilized, this project increases consumer awareness and guides consumers to learn about and decrease their electricity consumption to reduce the effects of global warming. To learn more about my project, please read my paper and look at my poster board.

After we complete our individual STEM project, we form teams to develop an aid for individuals with disabilites. This project carries on for the rest of the year. We partner with Seven Hills, a foundation that helps individuals with disabilities gain independence. My group decided to make a toothpaste dispenser. We were told that the current device costs an exorbitant amount. We decided we would build a cheaper and better prototype. The user complained of using only one toothpaste brand, so we designed the prototype to accept multiple toothpaste brands. Our prototype accepts Crest, Colgate, and Sensodyne. We 3D printed multiple prototypes and used a vacuum pump to dispense the toothpaste. The picture to the left shows our final prototype in vibrant yellow.
