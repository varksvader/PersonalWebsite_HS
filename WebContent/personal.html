I am currently a senior at the Massachusetts Academy of Math and Science at WPI. I live in Grafton, MA. My sending school is Grafton High. I have no siblings or pets, but this gives me more time to explore my interests. The reason why I came to Mass Academy is because I wanted to go in a STEM focused school. Since I was a kid, I always wanted to be an engineer, and Mass Academy gives me the opportunity to fulfill it.


Most people describe me as a short Indian female who has a really high voice. I don't think this is true, but my classmates say otherwise. Although I look skinny and flimsy, I can assure that I can handle myself. My math teacher Mr. Regele said I will hurt people by doing math. So if there's an invader on the loose, get him or her to me and hopefully he or she will have a black eye by the end of my interpretation on Heron's proof.

One of my favorite hobbies is hiking. When I was young, my parents and I would always travel to different parts of the country during my school vacations. I’ve been to the Smoky Mountains, Acadia, White Mountains, and many other mountain ranges. The tallest mountain I have climbed is Mount Washington when I was six years old, its height is 6,280 ft tall. This summer I went to Summer at Smith in Western Massachusetts, and climbed Mount Holyoke as pictured to the right. It was a fun experience although it was a very small mountain.





Another one of my hobbies is playing the piano. I have been playing for 10 years at Apple Tree Arts, located at the Grafton Common. I play many pieces like classical, jazz, contemporary, rock, Christmas Carols, and current pop music. My favorite classical composer is Mozart; I play his pieces often. I also play tennis. So far I’ve been on the varsity team for freshman and sophomore years, and hope to continue this year as well. I play in rotation for Second Doubles. I have lost some matches and won others. Whether I am good at it or not is questionable, but I have a lot fun being on the team. The girls are so nice and unique, it really gives me a chance to interact with people other than my friends. I guess you can say we’re like a tight knit family :)











For community service this year, I participated at the Community Barn at Grafton, which is located in my hometown. I picked and packaged locally grown fruits and vegetables. I mostly picked summer squash and cucumbers. At school, I also participated in e-NABLE, a program that builds prosthetic hands for children who need them. The eight of us made 16 hands, each of which cost $16.58 exactly in comparison to the expensive prosthetics that can go up to the thousands. We built the hands by modeling them in SolidWorks and 3D printing them at school. I am glad I did this project because it gave us insight on how our knowledge in science and technology helps other directly.

Although I have received education of coding during class, I also enjoy learning new code techniques during my free time. The programming languages I know so far are Java, Python, HTML and CSS, Javascript, Swift, and C/C++. My coding enables me to code multiple things, from websites to microcontrollers. This summer I coded a Tic Tac Toe Artifical Intelligence using Arduino. It was very successful, even though I struggled to find all the algorithms where the computer wins. Because I had fun with that, I've decided to continue to tinker with Arduino.

If you want to learn more about me or just want to talk about something on your mind, don't hesitate to email me at vsinha2@wpi.edu.
